Lead (plain language)
- Share willingly and receive according to what you actually give. Do not subsidize willful freeloading or harm.

Canonical text (author-supplied)
> From each as they will to each as they've given so all may eat save those refusing to contribute to their comfort and safety or whose actions destroy the comfort and safety of another.

Notes (selected)
- A cry sharpens vigilance; dependency clarifies need; burden highlights character.

Examples
- A neighbor regularly joins community meals and also takes weekly turns cooking/cleaning. In scarcity, shares proportionally to their ongoing contribution.
- A person who persistently disrupts safety is temporarily excluded while rebalancing occurs.

Common misunderstandings
- "Punishment for poverty": No — care and sustenance are due where contribution is not possible; refusal and destruction are different claims.

Practice guidance
- Define contribution clearly (time, skill, stewardship); publish expectations; track and revisit.
