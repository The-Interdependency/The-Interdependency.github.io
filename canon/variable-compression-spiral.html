Variable Compression Prime Spirals

PTCA/PCNA Artifacts Gallery — Stage 7

Core Concept

When the radius is allowed to grow by functions other than linear (+1 per rotation), powerful compression emerges. This allows visualization of primes at astronomically large scales within a compact visual field.

Mathematical Models

1. Doubling Radius per Rotation

r(n) = r₀ × 2k
θ(n) = 2π × {n / points(k)}
where k = floor(log₂(radius growth))

This produces exponential compression. Outer layers represent vastly larger numbers while maintaining visual coherence.

2. Inversion / Reciprocal Compression

r(n) = r₀ / (1 + α·n) or r(n) = r₀ / log(n+1)
(Circle inversion or harmonic compression)

Inversion maps large values into bounded space, creating dense clustering near the origin for very large primes.

3. Hybrid Log-Exponential Model

r(θ) = a·θ·log(θ) + b·2θ/2π

Current Observations

These compression schemes reveal persistent angular channels (prime rays) even at extreme scales. The 96-ray structure (mod 360) and 768-ray structure (mod 2880) appear to survive under multiple compression transforms, suggesting deep modular resonance in the prime distribution.

This work sits at the boundary between visualization, number theory, and tensor intuition. No elementary closed-form prime generation formula is known, but these patterns may illuminate underlying harmonic constraints on the primes.
