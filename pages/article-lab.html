Article One

Core Contract

From each as they will to each as they've given so all may eat save those refusing to contribute to their comfort and safety or whose actions destroy the comfort and safety of another.

Mnemonic: Contribute or step aside. No one eats from the labor of others without reciprocity.

Notes

  • A cry sharpens vigilance. Fragility elicits innovation.
  • Presence energizes dynamics. Dependency clarifies need.
  • Burden highlights character.

Watch for: The paper tiger. Malingering professionals. Greek gifts. Thieves. Red herrings. Narcissists. Empty calories. Liars. Celibate priests.

Article Two

No Slavery

None shall be enslaved; do nothing against your will, but feed the hungry, heal the sick, teach the kids, clean the mess, learn the Logics, cope the traumas, practice your art.

Mnemonic: Voluntary contribution only. Basic duties become art when done from the heart.

Low-Density Version

Feed the hungry • Heal the sick • Teach the kids • Clean the mess • Build the shelter • Defend the innocent • Learn what you do not know.

Practice: Wary of hungers which cannot be sated. Treat symptoms but balance systems. Who desires to learn can be taught.

Article Three

Reciprocity

Your speech demonstrates your desired mode of communication. As you act upon another, you demonstrate consent to be thus acted upon, even in reciprocity.

Mnemonic: Speak to be understood. Courtesy never disimproved a situation. An eye for an eye leaves the world blind, but a fist to the face might be kind.

Article Four

Transparency

None shall be left ignorant of the law; as you know, so shall you teach.

Mnemonic: Ignorance is not an excuse. Better honestly violent than dishonestly peaceful.

Where two argue and would fight, let it be done in Way Seers sight, between next day’s dawn and noon light, in competition all three agree each compete with parity.

Article Five

Adulthood

Adulthood ceases to be defined by an arbitrary age and is instead claimed by intentional action of demonstrated competence in a field of study.

Mnemonic: Claim adulthood by proclamation. Children learn, sophomores are dangerous, adults are dangerous without harm.
Claiming Adulthood:
1. Handwritten verbatim transcription of these Articles
2. Portfolio of demonstrable skills
3. Schedule of intended activities

Three States

Dependent → requires intercession
Independent → manages own survival
Interdependent → manages survival of others

Article Six

Violence & Authority

Violence is the ultimate Authority from which all other authority is derived and only ever determines who is left.

Mnemonic: No king goes to heaven. Twelve Adults empower one Citizen (aged 35+). Consult Way Seer before lethal force.

One who cannot laugh is in no fit state to wield violence or authority.

Article Seven

Definitions

Where any would converse, let them first define the terms; where a definition is absent or in dispute between Adults, a jury shall devise the standard.

Mnemonic: Define terms first. Way Seers hold no authority—only information beyond your reach.

Article Eight

Rights

The whole of the law is that what action harms none is a right; that what is done to preserve life according to and in balance with nature is a right; that what is done to resist coercion is a right; that what is done in the presence of informed consent is a right.

Mnemonic: Harm none. Preserve life. Resist coercion. Informed consent.
