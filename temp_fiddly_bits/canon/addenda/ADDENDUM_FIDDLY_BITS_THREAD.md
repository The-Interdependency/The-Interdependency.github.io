## A) Ephemeral Exchange (currency beneath money)

### A1. Definition
**Ephemeral Exchange** is the conversion of non-storable human contribution (labor, care, coordination, insight, craft, presence) into **durable continuities** required for life and flourishing.

**Currency (TIW):** not a token definition.  
Currency = **responsibility rendered → continuity secured**.  
Tokens/credits may exist only as accounting artifacts subordinate to the ledger.

### A2. Anti-abstraction constraint
If the system rewards *display* more than *maintenance*, it collapses into theater.

Maintenance work (repair, prevention, boring reliability, cleanup, support) is first-class currency.

## B) The Responsibility–Step Ratio (RSR)

### B1. Definition
RSR measures responsibility density embedded in any created thing by comparing:
- **Steps:** necessary discrete actions to produce safely
- **Spheres:** distinct responsibility domains that must be competently satisfied

\[
RSR = \frac{Spheres}{Steps}
\]

### B2. Interpretation (bands)
- ~1.0: individually producible with competent provisioning
- 1.2–2.5: cooperative artifact; distributed competence
- ≥3.0: civilizational artifact; requires institutions, standards, coordinated competence

### B3. Counting discipline (anti-gaming)
RSR is a diagnostic, not an oracle. It is sensitive to counting choices.

- **Step:** count at the level where a mistake creates a distinct class of failure.
- **Sphere:** count at the level where accountability meaningfully changes hands:
  distinct obligation + distinct competence threshold + distinct externalities if mishandled.

**Publication discipline:** any published RSR must state
- step granularity used
- sphere taxonomy version used
- verification method used
- known externalities excluded (if any)

## C) Adult Endowment (AE) — threshold coherence

### C1. Definition
The **Adult Endowment** is a one-time, unconditional provisioning granted upon claiming adulthood.
It installs standing and minimum stability required to enter interdependence as a responsible actor.

AE is recognition of membership, not merit.

### C2. Components (canonical categories)
1) Essential survival tools (durable, portable, repairable; climate-appropriate)  
2) Knowledge core (physical + offline digital; replicable by design)  
3) Non-tradable standing credentials (access keys; not currency; non-seizable)  
4) Health baseline (assessment + stabilization; function restoration, not enhancement)  
5) Responsibility primer (stewardship, reliability, cooperation orientation)

### C3. Replacement and loss (non-punitive)
AE is “one-time” as provisioning logic, but reality includes theft, loss, disaster, sabotage.

- Essentials may be replaced under documented loss.
- Repeat loss triggers a **supportive audit** focused on threat environment and capability gaps, not moral blame.

### C4. Anti-coercion / non-seizure
No institution, employer, household, faction, or family unit may:
- confiscate AE components,
- condition AE access on obedience, debt, or affiliation,
- require repayment as a prerequisite for standing.

Violation constitutes extraction from the threshold (attack on the cooperative order).

## D) Two-layer economy boundary (continuity vs preference)

### D1. Continuity layer (non-negotiable)
Baseline continuity is not a market.
Subsistence-level access to survival-critical domains is provisioned by the cooperative order via responsibility and standing.

### D2. Preference layer (negotiable)
Currency persists for non-survival domains where preference and differentiation legitimately exist:
- objects d’art
- noncritical tools beyond subsistence
- optional services
- many digital artifacts (subject to boundary rule)

### D3. No re-entry (hard constraint)
No currency instrument may gate survival, standing, or the threshold conditions of adulthood.
No “pay or lose standing.” No survival-rent extraction.

## E) “What one requires for survival, another a toy” — boundary rule

### E1. Necessity is conditional
“Survival-critical” is not a property of an object. It is a property of **object-in-context**.

Context axes:
- environment (climate, terrain, hazards)
- biology (disability, chronic conditions, sensory limits)
- threat model (violence/coercion risk)
- infrastructure presence/absence (water, power, shelter, medical access)
- role/sphere load (caretaker, medic, operator)
- time horizon (hours/days vs weeks/months)

### E2. Continuity classification test (person/context-specific)
An item belongs in the Continuity Layer for a given person/context if removing it causes any of:

1) Immediate failure (death, severe injury, acute medical collapse)  
2) Short-horizon collapse (loss of shelter/water/food safety within days)  
3) Standing collapse (loss of access credentials required to obtain baseline continuity)  
4) Safety collapse (predictable exposure to violence/coercion without substitute)  
5) Function collapse (baseline self-maintenance becomes impossible without assistive tool)  

Otherwise it belongs in the Preference Layer.

### E3. Digital artifacts (toy vs tool clause)
Digital artifacts default to Preference Layer, except when the classification test is triggered by:
- communication needed to access continuity services
- safety/threat requirements (coercion avoidance, emergency coordination)
- disability access tools
- identity/standing verification required for baseline services

## F) Markets, exchange, investment (subordinate to continuity)

### F1. Market potentials
Markets are permitted as coordination layers that route effort toward unmet needs.
A “market opportunity” is a measurable continuity gap closable by competent action within accountable spheres.

### F2. Currency exchange
Exchange exists to translate across responsibility ledgers/jurisdictions.
All exchange instruments must remain auditable against delivered continuity and accounted externalities.
Speculative accumulation detached from continuity delivery is noncompliant.

### F3. Individual investment
Investment is allocation of surplus capacity into higher-RSR continuities (infrastructure, training, reserves, institutions, tooling).
Returns are legitimate only insofar as they reflect maintained continuity and do not externalize harm.
